About Electronic Signature

What is electronic signature and why is it required?

A guarantee of integrity of origin and content is currently only required by law in respect of electronically-transmitted documents that are intended to function as VAT invoices, or as supporting documentation for invoices.  The reason for this is that such documents may require inspection by local tax authorities some time in the future and the tax authorities must be sure that no changes have been made to the invoices that would affect VAT liability.  Documents that do not serve this function therefore do not legally require signature, though e-signature may be used more widely in the future in respect of many types of documents.

What will I still receive paper copies of some invoices?

Currently, only invoices generated by our DPC supplier for distribution through BSPLink can be electronically signed.  You will therefore receive paper copies of the remaining, BSP-generated, invoices.  Although advance copies of the invoice may be made available electronically, only the paper copies function as acceptable VAT invoices and therefore must be retained.  In the future, it is intended that all IATA invoices will be electronically signed, at which point paper copies will no longer be necessary.

Do I have to keep a paper copy of my electronic invoices?

 No, it is not necessary to retain a paper copy of electronically signed invoices but you must save the electronic version.  If you are subject to a tax inspection at a later date the tax authorities would only want to see the electronically signed document.
