About Electronic Signature

What is electronic signature and why is it required?

The electronic signature provides a guarantee of integrity of origin and content.  It is currently only required by law for electronically transmitted documents that are intended to function as invoices, or as supporting documentation for invoices for VAT purposes.
The electronic signature provides audit evidence for local tax authorities to verify VAT paid. Documents that do not serve this function therefore do not legally require an electronic signature.

What will I still receive paper copies of some invoices?

Currently, all IATA invoices are electronically signed and are posted myIATA. Paper copies will not be automatically provided. The customer has the option to request a paper invoice. You will continue to receive paper invoices if electronic signatures are not yet available in your market.  Electronic invoices without an electronic signature are not valid invoices for VAT purposes.

Do I have to keep a paper copy of my electronic invoices?

When legally adopted in your country, it is not necessary to retain an additional paper copy of electronically signed invoice.   You must save the electronic version for future tax inspections.
